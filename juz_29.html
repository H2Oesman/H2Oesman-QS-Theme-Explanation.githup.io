Juz 29 - Analisis Tematik

Sumber: IslamicStudies.info

Gambaran Umum Juz 29

Juz 29 mencakup halaman 562 hingga 581 dalam Mushaf Al-Quran standar. Juz ini terdiri dari 11 surah Makkiyah: Surah Al-Mulk (ayat 1-30), Surah Al-Qalam (ayat 1-52), Surah Al-Haaqqah (ayat 1-52), Surah Al-Ma'arij (ayat 1-44), Surah Nuh (ayat 1-28), Surah Al-Jinn (ayat 1-28), Surah Al-Muzzammil (ayat 1-20), Surah Al-Muddaththir (ayat 1-56), Surah Al-Qiyamah (ayat 1-40), Surah Al-Insan (ayat 1-31), dan Surah Al-Mursalat (ayat 1-50).

Juz ini dikenal dengan nama "Tabārakalladzī" yang diambil dari awal ayat 1 Surah Al-Mulk. Tema utama yang menonjol dalam juz ini adalah keesaan Allah, hari kebangkitan, kisah para nabi, dan peringatan bagi orang-orang kafir.

Hizb: Hizb 57-58

Surah dalam Juz ini:

  • Surah Al-Mulk (Kerajaan) - Ayat 1-30
  • Surah Al-Qalam (Pena) - Ayat 1-52
  • Surah Al-Haaqqah (Hari Kiamat) - Ayat 1-52
  • Surah Al-Ma'arij (Tempat-tempat Naik) - Ayat 1-44
  • Surah Nuh - Ayat 1-28
  • Surah Al-Jinn (Jin) - Ayat 1-28
  • Surah Al-Muzzammil (Orang yang Berselimut) - Ayat 1-20
  • Surah Al-Muddaththir (Orang yang Berkemul) - Ayat 1-56
  • Surah Al-Qiyamah (Hari Kiamat) - Ayat 1-40
  • Surah Al-Insan (Manusia) - Ayat 1-31
  • Surah Al-Mursalat (Malaikat yang Diutus) - Ayat 1-50

Juz 29 - Thematic Analysis

Sources: IslamicStudies.info

Overview of Juz 29

Juz 29 covers pages 562 to 581 in the standard Quran Mushaf. This Juz consists of 11 Meccan surahs: Surah Al-Mulk (verses 1-30), Surah Al-Qalam (verses 1-52), Surah Al-Haaqqah (verses 1-52), Surah Al-Ma'arij (verses 1-44), Surah Nuh (verses 1-28), Surah Al-Jinn (verses 1-28), Surah Al-Muzzammil (verses 1-20), Surah Al-Muddaththir (verses 1-56), Surah Al-Qiyamah (verses 1-40), Surah Al-Insan (verses 1-31), and Surah Al-Mursalat (verses 1-50).

This Juz is known as "Tabārakalladzī" taken from the beginning of verse 1 of Surah Al-Mulk. The prominent themes are the oneness of Allah, the Day of Resurrection, stories of prophets, and warnings to disbelievers.

Hizb: Hizb 57-58

Surahs in this Juz:

  • Surah Al-Mulk (The Sovereignty) - Verses 1-30
  • Surah Al-Qalam (The Pen) - Verses 1-52
  • Surah Al-Haaqqah (The Reality) - Verses 1-52
  • Surah Al-Ma'arij (The Ascending Stairways) - Verses 1-44
  • Surah Nuh - Verses 1-28
  • Surah Al-Jinn (The Jinn) - Verses 1-28
  • Surah Al-Muzzammil (The Enshrouded One) - Verses 1-20
  • Surah Al-Muddaththir (The Cloaked One) - Verses 1-56
  • Surah Al-Qiyamah (The Resurrection) - Verses 1-40
  • Surah Al-Insan (Man) - Verses 1-31

Surah Al-Mulk (67:1-30)

تَبَٰرَكَ ٱلَّذِى بِيَدِهِ ٱلْمُلْكُ وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍ قَدِيرٌ
Tabārakallażī biyadihil-mulku wa huwa 'alā kulli syai`in qadīr.

"Blessed is He in whose hand is dominion, and He is over all things competent." (Al-Mulk 67:1)

Overview of Surah Al-Mulk

Surah Al-Mulk (سورة الملك) is the 67th chapter, consisting of 30 verses, all included in Juz 29. Classified as Meccan, it is named Al-Mulk (The Sovereignty) because it discusses Allah's power and sovereignty over the universe. According to IslamicStudies.info, this surah was revealed after Surah At-Tahrim. It explains that this surah affirms Allah's power in creating the heavens and earth, and gives warnings about the punishment of Hell. It emphasizes messages about Allah's greatness, the wonders of creation, and the importance of gratitude.

Message of Aqidah, Worship, & Akhlaq

Essence of Surah Al-Mulk Teachings

  • Aqidah (Creed) : Belief that Allah owns the kingdom of heavens and earth (67:1), faith in the Day of Resurrection (67:2), conviction that Allah knows all secrets (67:13).
  • Worship : Glorify Allah (67:1), seek forgiveness (67:12), be grateful for Allah's blessings (67:23).
  • Akhlaq (Character) : Reflect on Allah's creation (67:3-4), do not be arrogant (67:21), be accountable for actions (67:10-11).

Overview of Surah Al-Qalam

Surah Al-Qalam (سورة القلم) is the 68th chapter, consisting of 52 verses, all included in Juz 29. Classified as Meccan, it is named Al-Qalam (The Pen) because it begins with Allah's oath by the pen. According to IslamicStudies.info, this surah was revealed after Surah Al-'Alaq. It explains that this surah defends Prophet Muhammad from accusations of disbelievers, and gives a parable about the stingy garden owner. It emphasizes messages about Prophet Muhammad's character, warning against stinginess, and promise of Paradise for the righteous.

It explains that this surah describes the terrors of the Day of Judgment, the fate of deniers, and the truth of the Quran. It emphasizes messages about the certainty of the Day of Judgment, recompense for believers and disbelievers, and the authenticity of the Quran.

It emphasizes messages about the Day of Judgment, human characteristics, and reward for those who pray.

Overview of Surah Nuh

Surah Nuh (سورة نوح) is the 71st chapter, consisting of 28 verses, all included in Juz 29. Classified as Meccan, it is named Nuh because it narrates Prophet Noah's preaching to his people. According to IslamicStudies.info, this surah was revealed after Surah An-Nahl. It explains that this surah tells of Noah's patience in preaching for 950 years, his people's rejection, and Noah's prayer for their destruction. It emphasizes messages about patience in preaching, consequences of disbelief, and Allah's power to save believers.

Verses 25-28 Punishment and Forgiveness Prayer Noah's people drowned; Noah's prayer for forgiveness for himself and believers.

Message of Aqidah, Worship, & Akhlaq

Essence of Surah Nuh Teachings

  • Aqidah (Creed) : Belief that Allah sends prophets (71:1), faith in Allah's power to save believers (71:26), conviction in punishment for disbelievers (71:25).
  • Worship : Pray to Allah (71:26-28), seek forgiveness (71:28), be grateful to Allah (71:3).
  • Akhlaq (Character) : Patient in preaching (71:5-9), not arrogant (71:7), gentle in approach (71:9), pray for believers' forgiveness (71:28).

قُلْ أُوحِىَ إِلَىَّ أَنَّهُ ٱسْتَمَعَ نَفَرٌ مِّنَ ٱلْجِنِّ فَقَالُوٓا۟ إِنَّا سَمِعْنَا قُرْءَانًا عَجَبًا
Qul ụḥiya ilayya annahusta ma'a nafarum minal-jinni fa qālū innā sami'nā qur`ānan 'ajabā.

"Say, [O Muhammad], 'It has been revealed to me that a group of the jinn listened and said, 'Indeed, we have heard an amazing Quran.'" (Al-Jinn 72:1)

Overview of Surah Al-Jinn

Surah Al-Jinn (سورة الجن) is the 72nd chapter, consisting of 28 verses, all included in Juz 29. Classified as Meccan, it is named Al-Jinn because it narrates a group of jinn listening to the Quran and believing. According to IslamicStudies.info, this surah was revealed after Surah Al-A'raf. It explains that this surah tells of jinn who heard Prophet Muhammad reciting the Quran, then they believed and conveyed it to their people. It emphasizes messages about Allah's oneness, the Quran's function as guidance, and prohibition of associating partners with Allah.

Overview of Surah Al-Muzzammil

Surah Al-Muzzammil (سورة المزمل) is the 73rd chapter, consisting of 20 verses, all included in Juz 29. Classified as Meccan, it is named Al-Muzzammil (The Enshrouded One) because it begins with Allah's call to Prophet Muhammad who was wrapping himself. According to IslamicStudies.info, this surah was revealed after Surah Al-Qalam. It explains that this surah contains commands to rise at night, recite the Quran with measured recitation, and warnings about coming punishment. It emphasizes messages about the virtue of night prayer, patience, and warning to disbelievers.

"I swear by the Day of Resurrection." (Al-Qiyama 75:1)

Overview of Surah Al-Qiyama

Surah Al-Qiyama (سورة القيامة) is the 75th chapter, consisting of 40 verses, all included in Juz 29. Classified as Meccan, it is named Al-Qiyama (The Resurrection) because it discusses events of the Day of Resurrection. According to IslamicStudies.info, this surah was revealed after Surah Al-Qari'ah. It explains that this surah describes the process of human resurrection from graves, gathering in the plain of Judgment, and Allah's ability to revive bones. It emphasizes messages about the certainty of the Day of Judgment, Allah's power to revive the dead, and warning for the heedless.

Surah Al-Insan

Surah Al-Insan (76:1-31)

هَلْ أَتَىٰ عَلَى ٱلْإِنسَٰنِ حِينٌ مِّنَ ٱلدَّهْرِ لَمْ يَكُن شَيْـًٔا مَّذْكُورًا
Hal atā 'alal-insāni ḥīnum minad-dahri lam yakun syai`am mażkụrā.

"Has there [not] come upon man a period of time when he was not a thing [even] mentioned?" (Al-Insan 76:1)

Overview of Surah Al-Insan

Surah Al-Insan (سورة الإنسان) is the 76th chapter, consisting of 31 verses, all included in Juz 29. Classified as Medinan according to some opinions, it is named Al-Insan (The Man) because it discusses the origin of human creation. According to IslamicStudies.info, this surah was revealed after Surah Ar-Rahman. It explains that this surah narrates the story of the Prophet's household who fasted three days and fed the poor, and describes Paradise delights. It emphasizes messages about human origin, reward for the patient, and delights of Paradise.

It explains that this surah contains Allah's oaths, warnings about the Day of Judgment, and descriptions of the terror of the Day of Recompense. It emphasizes messages about the certainty of the Day of Judgment, punishment for deniers, and warning to be righteous.
